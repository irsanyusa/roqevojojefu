    The year is 1989, one of Michael Jordan‘s most iconic moments is simply called “The Shot.” The shoes he was wearing when he hit the game-winning basket over Craig Ehlo and the Cleveland Cavaliers were the Nike Air Jordan 4 sneakers.

    And to celebrate the 35th anniversary of that historic moment in Michael Jordan’s basketball history (May 7, 1989), Nike is re-releasing the Air Jordan 4 sneakers in a new North Carolina “blue industry” colorway for a new generation.

    $215Buy: Air Jordan 4 Retro

    Retailing for $215, the Nike Air Jordan 4 sneakers are made from premium leather and suede-link Nubuck with a clean look and feel that’s retro, yet modern. They feature the “Jumpman” logo on their tongues, eye-catching netting, “Nike Air” logo on their heels, transparent air bubble in their midsoles and prominent wings on their collars that look classic — even though this design is 35 years old.
